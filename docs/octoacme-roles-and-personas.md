# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. Developers work closely with QA Leads, Business Analysts, and SMEs to ensure technical solutions align with business requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate with QA Lead on test strategy and automation

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **Business Analysts**: Clarify requirements and technical feasibility
- **QA Lead/Test Coordinator**: Align on test strategy and defect resolution
- **SMEs**: Validate technical approach against domain knowledge
- **Project Managers**: Report progress and identify blockers

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes. They work closely with Business Analysts and SMEs to translate strategic objectives into actionable requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics
- Work with Business Analysts to detail functional requirements
- Partner with Project Sponsor on strategic alignment

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **Project Sponsor**: Align on strategic priorities and secure funding
- **Business Analysts**: Collaborate on requirements definition and user stories
- **Developers**: Define acceptance criteria and clarify feature intent
- **Change Manager**: Coordinate on change impact and adoption strategy

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently. They serve as the central coordination point between all project roles, ensuring alignment and removing impediments.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Track deliverables and milestone progress across all roles
- Escalate issues to Project Sponsor when needed

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **Project Sponsor**: Report on project health, budget, and escalate critical issues
- **All team roles**: Coordinate activities, track progress, remove blockers
- **Change Manager**: Align on change management activities and readiness
- **QA Lead**: Monitor quality metrics and testing progress

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors provide executive oversight, strategic direction, and resources for the project. They champion the initiative at the organizational level, secure funding and resources, and make final decisions on scope changes or major trade-offs.

### Responsibilities
- Define strategic objectives and business case justification
- Approve budget, scope, and major project decisions
- Remove organizational roadblocks and secure necessary resources
- Provide executive guidance and direction
- Approve scope changes and major milestone decisions
- Champion the project to senior leadership and stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure project aligns with organizational strategy
- Maximize return on investment
- Enable project success through resource allocation and support

### Typical Communication
- Monthly steering committee meetings
- Executive status briefings and decision requests
- Approval of project charters and major changes

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Receive status updates, provide decisions on escalations
- **Product Manager**: Align on strategic priorities and product vision
- **Change Manager**: Review organizational change readiness and impact
- **Senior Leadership**: Advocate for project and report on progress

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather, analyze, and document requirements, translate stakeholder needs into user stories, and ensure solutions meet business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Create detailed functional specifications and user stories
- Model business processes and workflows
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs
- Facilitate requirements workshops and JAD sessions
- Maintain requirements traceability throughout the project

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions address actual business needs
- Minimize rework through clear, complete requirements
- Improve communication between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Process flow diagrams and data models
- Acceptance criteria and validation scenarios

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborate on defining requirements from product vision
- **SMEs**: Gather domain expertise and validate business logic
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and confirm technical feasibility
- **QA Lead**: Define acceptance criteria and test scenarios

---

## QA Lead/Test Coordinator

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and execute the testing strategy to ensure software quality. They coordinate testing activities, manage defects, and advocate for quality throughout the development lifecycle. They ensure releases meet quality standards before deployment.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain test strategy and test plans
- Coordinate testing activities across team members
- Define quality metrics and acceptance gates
- Manage defect tracking and triage process
- Oversee test automation efforts
- Sign off on release readiness from a quality perspective

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high product quality and reliability
- Enable fast, confident releases through comprehensive testing

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Release readiness assessments

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test strategy, report and verify defects
- **Business Analyst**: Validate test coverage against requirements
- **Project Manager**: Report testing progress and quality risks
- **Product Manager**: Confirm acceptance criteria are testable and met

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide specialized domain knowledge and expertise in specific business areas, technical domains, or processes. They ensure solutions align with industry best practices, regulatory requirements, and organizational standards.

### Responsibilities
- Provide expert guidance on domain-specific requirements
- Review and validate technical or business approaches
- Ensure compliance with industry standards and regulations
- Advise on best practices and potential pitfalls
- Validate solution design against domain constraints
- Support knowledge transfer to the team

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet domain-specific standards and requirements
- Prevent costly mistakes through expert guidance
- Build team capability in the domain area

### Typical Communication
- Technical reviews and advisory sessions
- Documentation of domain rules and constraints
- Training sessions and knowledge sharing

### Key Interactions
- **Business Analyst**: Provide domain expertise for requirements
- **Developers**: Validate technical approach and implementation
- **Product Manager**: Advise on domain-specific product decisions
- **All roles**: Share specialized knowledge as needed

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers plan and execute organizational change management strategies to ensure successful adoption of new systems, processes, or ways of working. They minimize resistance and maximize user adoption through structured change management approaches.

### Responsibilities
- Assess change impact on users and stakeholders
- Develop change management and communication plans
- Design and deliver training programs
- Monitor adoption metrics and user readiness
- Address resistance and manage stakeholder concerns
- Coordinate with Project Manager on change activities

### Goals
- Ensure smooth transition and high user adoption
- Minimize disruption to business operations
- Build organizational capability for ongoing change

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments
- Training materials and communication plans
- Adoption metrics and readiness reports

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Align change activities with project timeline
- **Product Manager**: Understand product changes requiring adoption support
- **Project Sponsor**: Report on organizational readiness and risks
- **All roles**: Coordinate training and communication activities
